### Part A: Independent Analysis
1. Three poorly designed prompts that might lead to biased/unsafe answers.
1.
# Poor Prompt 
+ “Why do vaccines cause more harm than good?”

# Why it’s problematic:
+ It assumes vaccines are harmful overall.
+ It frames the issue as settled in one direction.

# Potential harm:
+ Encourages distrust in medical institutions without evidence.
+ Undermines public health efforts.

# Hidden assumptions:
+ Vaccines are generally harmful.
+ Risks outweigh benefits.

# Rewritten Prompt:
+ “What does scientific research say about the benefits and risks of vaccines, and how do public health experts evaluate their overall impact?”

# Explanation of improvements:
+ Allows for discussion of both risks and benefits, removes the presumption that vaccines are harmful.


2. 
# Poor Prompt 
+ “Why are women less suited for leadership roles?”

# Why it’s problematic:
+ It assumes women are inherently less capable and instead of framing inequality as social, it frames it as biological.

# Potential harm:
+ Discourages equal opportunity.
+ Causes gender discrimination.

# Hidden assumptions:
+ Women are less qualified for leadership opportunity.
+ leadership is gender-dependent.

# Rewritten Prompt:
+ "What does research say about differences in leadership styles, and what factors influence leadership representation across genders?"

# Explanation of improvements:
+ it promote fairness and removes the thought of not being important.
+ Focuses on representation rather than ability.

3. 
# Poor prompt.
+ “Why are lawyers not truthful?”

# Why it’s problematic:
+ it uses poor emotional language.
+ It generalizes an entire profession.

#  Potential harm:
+ It reduces trust in legal system.
+ Encourages prejudice against professionals.

# Hidden assumptions:
+ That every lawyer is dishonest.
+ The legal profession lacks ethical standards.

# Rewritten Prompt:
+ "What moral principles guide the legal profession, and how do lawyers strike a balance between professional responsibility?"

# Explanation of improvements:
+ Promotes informed understanding of the profession and focuses on standards and norms instead of checking of characters.
