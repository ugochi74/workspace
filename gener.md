### Practice creative writing through prompt.

# step 1: Choose a Theme

# I selected two themes:

* Friendship

* Overcoming Fear

# Step 2: Write a Simple Generative Prompt

# Prompt 1:

* Write a short story about friendship in under 300 words.

# Prompt 2:

* Write a short story about overcoming fear in under 300 words.


# Step 3: Test the Prompts
# Output 1 – Theme: Friendship

 Precious had never liked the first day of school. New desks. New faces. New reasons to feel invisible.

She sat alone at lunch, tracing circles on her tray, when someone dropped into the seat across from her.

“Is this seat taken?” the girl asked, already smiling.

Precious shook her head.

“I’m Melody. I talk a lot when I’m nervous.”

That made Precious laugh — just a little.

By the end of lunch, they had discovered a shared love for mystery novels and strawberry milk. By the end of the week, they were walking home together. By the end of the month, silence between them felt comfortable instead of awkward.

When Precious failed her math test, Melody stayed on the phone for two hours helping her study. When Melody didn’t make the soccer team, Precious showed up with cookies and terrible jokes.

Years later, when people asked them how long they had been friends, they didn't remember the exact day.

It felt like they had always chosen each other.

And somehow, they still did.


# Output 2 – Theme: Overcoming Fear

 The diving board looked higher than it had yesterday.

 Clement's toes curled over the edge. The pool shimmered below him, bright and impossibly far away.

“Just jump!” someone shouted.

He couldn’t.

His chest tightened. What if he slipped? What if he landed wrong? What if everyone laughed?

He climbed down.

That night, he couldn’t stop thinking about it — not the height, but the feeling of walking away.

The next day, he climbed again.

His heart pounded just as hard. The water still looked far.

But this time, he counted.

One.

Two.

Three.

Before his mind could interrupt, he bent his knees and jumped.

For a second, there was only air.

Then water rushed around him — cool, shocking, real.

When he surfaced, no one was laughing.

They were cheering.

Clement smiled, pushing wet hair from his eyes.

The board was still high.

But it no longer felt impossible.
